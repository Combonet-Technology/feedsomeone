Our Impact

Grassroots action since 2019

Founder-funded, volunteer-supported, and now becoming grant-ready

OEF's public impact record begins with Feed Someone, a volunteer-led relief movement that started in Akure in 2019. We are publishing this history carefully: completed work is separated from planned programmes, and uncertain counts or institution details are clearly marked for verification.

Feed Someone 1.0

27 December 2019 · Akure, Ondo State

Friends and volunteers prepared meals, bought drinks, and distributed them to an underserved street-connected community. The outreach reached approximately 150+ people.

Support delivered: cooked food, drinks, personal volunteer mobilisation.

View gallery

Feed Someone 2.0

December 2020 · Akure, Ondo State

The outreach expanded from community feeding into institution-based relief. Two children's homes and an underserved street-connected community received practical support.

Support delivered: staple food items, baby-care supplies, hygiene and household essentials, clothing, footwear, and other practical relief materials gathered through volunteer and workplace mobilisation.

View gallery

Collaborative outreach support

2021 · Abeokuta, Ogun State

The founder participated in or supported an independently led outreach reported to have reached approximately 200 children. Exact date, partner details, and OEF's role are being verified before stronger public attribution.

Orphanage Relief

9 August 2022 · Lugbe / Airport Road axis, Abuja

A founder-funded visit delivered food items and practical relief supplies to a children's home with help from a small group of friends. The institution name and beneficiary count are pending confirmation.

Partner-led rural outreach support

2025-2026 · Nigeria

The founder has also supported partner-led rural outreach activities involving food items, clothing, medicine, and medical checks. These contributions are being documented internally and will be published with exact figures only where receipts, partner consent, and records support public attribution.

Impact records are being strengthened as OEF prepares for responsible first-grant partnerships. Where exact beneficiary records are unavailable, we use cautious wording and identify gaps clearly.
